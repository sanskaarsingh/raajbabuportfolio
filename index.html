04 // THE STORY

ORIGIN & INFLUENCE

Born in Bihar and raised in the rhythms of village life, Raaj Babu’s earliest years grounded him in the local culture and the everyday struggles of his people. Later moving to Jamshedpur in his adolescence, he carried this heritage with him. It was in high school that he discovered rap, a medium that felt like meditation, a cathartic outlet far more honest than poetry or polite songwriting. What started as rhymes among friends soon grew into a deep commitment to representation.

But he’s not just a solo act - he’s a builder. In Jamshedpur’s burgeoning underground-hip-hop movement, Raaj Babu helped pioneer a coming-together of rappers, beat-boxers, graffiti artists, dancers, and other hip-hop enthusiasts under one roof. Through events, battles, show-cases and community-driven gigs, he worked to bring together a scattered scene and gave it a voice.

Musically, he’s bold. He experiments fusing jazz, funk, Bhojpuri idioms, and hip-hop energy to create something that feels fresh yet rooted, progressive yet respectful of tradition. That blend of styles, languages and sensibilities helps him challenge stereotypes about “what Bhojpuri music is meant to be.” Instead of cliches, he offers complexity. Instead of trend-following, he offers self.

VISION 2025-2027

Raaj Babu’s vision as an artist is to turn his real-life struggle into sound; to prove that consistency, hard work, and raw honesty can build a legacy. He sees music not just as entertainment, but as a voice for every young dreamer who comes from small cities and wants to rise without shortcuts. Through Bhojpuri rap, he aims to show the world that authenticity is stronger than gimmicks, and that staying rooted in your culture is a superpower, not a limitation. His vision is to inspire, to uplift, and to create art that reflects the truth of his journey; unfiltered, fearless, and forever evolving.
